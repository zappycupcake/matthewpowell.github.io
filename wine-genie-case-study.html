01About Wine Genie

Wine Genie is a personalised subscription service from Naked Wines, offering new customers the chance to explore new wines and regions tailored to their tastes.

Buying wine is no easy task; colour, style and country can make it a frustrating experience for the average wine drinker. Wine Genie aims to reduce the effort involved in the wine buying process.

The journey for new Wine Genie customers has already proven successful. Users begin with a quiz, answering questions on their wine knowledge and wine preferences. This builds their wine profile and encourages them to sign up to Wine Genie.

02The problem

Wine Genie is working for new customers, why is it not working for existing?

There are 1.2 million US customers who have tried Naked Wines but not continued to buy. If they liked our wines, but struggled to know what to choose thereafter, could Wine Genie entice these dormant customers to spend and the become valuable LTV customers?

Customer interviews revealed that the existing landing page didn't explain the Wine Genie proposition clearly enough.

Existing customer landing page

03Wireframing

Designing the wireframes

If we're able to communicate the key benefits of Wine Genie clearly, can we improve the chances of these dormant customers entering the Wine Genie quiz, subscribing to the product and increasing their LTV?

With a rebrand in process; I took the opportunity to incorporate the new design elements to enhance the brand proposition and make the page visually more appealing.

These wireframes were created to validate the solution and encourage discussions about the layout during user interviews. I developed three designs, each showcasing the same content but arranged in a different hierarchy.

Wireframes

04User testing

Designing the prototypes

Once the wireframes were internally critiqued and refined, I created high fidelity versions for all three designs, focusing on mobile first before moving onto tablet and then desktop.

We conducted 30 customer interviews, 10 for each variant.

From these customer interviews, we found that they appreciated the design of the page, how easy it was to digest the USP, trust in the product and the quality it offers.

05Learnings

Learnings and the winning variants

Key insight 1

Users needed to understand the 'how it works' before telling them the benefits in order to give to give them the confidence to take action.

When we lead with 'how it works; (V1 & V3) customers had a better understanding: “I can edit at any point in time. And that it will give me wines that I would enjoy based off my quiz.” As a result they were more likely to be open to the idea of signing up.

Key insight 2

People understood Wine Genie fully after reading the 1,2,3 step.

Simplifying the steps down to a 1,2,3 made it easier to understand. A cleaner UI followed by concise and instructive titles helped users 'get it' much quicker.

Key insight 3

The landing pages set the tone/brand personality for the whole experience.

With good user experience and succinct content design, we can prime customers to be more open to the idea of signing up, when they first enter the quiz. We can also do this by building trust in Wine Genie through external validation, like the USA Today award. .

06Metrics and Success

What does success look like?

It's still earlier days, but the results speak for themselves.

Among our existing customer base, there were 15,000 unique views, a substantial figure. Notably, 72% of these customers engaged with the quiz and proceeded to explore the case page. Following this, 23% of customers demonstrated further interest by adding items to their basket. Finally, an impressive 8% of these individuals made the decision to subscribe to Wine Genie.
